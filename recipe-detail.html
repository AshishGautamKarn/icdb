Classic Lasagna Bolognese

By Chef Anna Rossi | Published: Oct 27, 2023
8.8/10 (1,234 votes)
Taste: 9.2 | Ease: 7.5 | Authenticity: 9.0 | Visual: 8.5

A rich and meaty lasagna featuring layers of slow-cooked bolognese sauce, creamy béchamel, fresh pasta sheets, and generous amounts of Parmesan and Mozzarella cheese. A true Italian classic perfect for family dinners.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb Ground Beef (80/20 lean)
  • 1 large Onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves Garlic, minced
  • 28 oz Crushed Tomatoes
  • 1 box Lasagna Noodles (oven-ready recommended)

  2. Techniques: Simmering

Details & Information

  • Total Time: 2 hours 15 minutes

Trivia & Fun Facts

  • The earliest known recipe resembling modern lasagna dates back to Naples in the Middle Ages.
  • Bolognese sauce traditionally includes milk or cream for richness.

Common Goofs (Community Sourced)

  • Not letting the lasagna rest before slicing (results in a sloppy serve).
  • Using no-boil noodles without enough sauce moisture (can result in dry noodles).
  • Overcooking the bolognese, making it too dry.

Tips, Notes & Variations

Chef's Tips

  • For a richer flavor, use a mix of beef and pork in the bolognese.
  • Let the bolognese simmer for longer (up to 2-3 hours) for deeper flavor development.
  • Use fresh mozzarella for a superior melt and taste.

Questions & Answers

Q: Can I make this ahead of time?

A: Yes! You can assemble the lasagna completely, cover tightly, and refrigerate for up to 2 days before baking. You might need to add 10-15 minutes to the initial baking time if baking from cold. - Chef Anna Rossi
